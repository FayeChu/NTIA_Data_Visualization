Background

NTIA’s BroadbandUSA team and Washington State Office of the CIO are collaborating with graduate students in the University of Washington iSchool to improve the accessibility and use of broadband data supporting local decision makers. This work leverages available and emerging open datasets, explore new data analysis and filtering, and new visual representations designed to support broadband leaders.

Research Partners

Our initial research partner is the Office of the CIO (OCIO) for the State of Washington, working in conjunction with graduate students from the Master of Science in Information Management (MSIM) at the iSchool. MSIM teams of 2 to 5 students will work from January through May, completing projects in May 2018.

Later, we plan to add partnerships with additional States such as the Virginia Center for Innovative Technology. Federal partners include the Federal Communications Commission, U.S. Census Bureau, and NTIA’s Office of Policy and Analysis (OPAD).
